Dr.Sanduk Ruit

The Eye Surgeon giving Sight and Hope to the World's poor: The GOD of Sight.

Here's the major Accomplishments and Works of his life:

  • 1986 - Ruit and Fred Hollows developed a strategy for using inexpensive intraocular lenses to bring small-incision cataract surgery to the developing world.
  • 1994 - Ruit and The Fred Hollows Foundation founded the Tilganga Eye Center, now called the Tilganga Institute of Ophthalmology, in Kathmandu. Tilganga has performed over 90,000 operations and trained over 500 medical personnel from around the world, and produces Ruit's intraocular lenses at a cost of less than US$5 each. It also produces prosthetic eyes for US$3, compared to imports that cost $150.
  • 1995 - Ruit developed a new intraocular lens that could be produced far more cheaply.
  • 2006 - After treating a North Korean diplomat in Kathmandu, Ruit persuaded North Korean authorities to let him visit.There he conducted surgery on 1000 patients and trained many local surgeons.
  • Also, - For those unable to reach the Center, or who live in otherwise isolated rural areas, Ruit and his team set up mobile eye camps, often using tents, classrooms, and even animal stables as makeshift operating rooms.
  • Also, - Out of the Darkness, a 2011 film by Italian director Stefano Levi, documents Ruit's work in remote Northern Nepal.

Here's the Awards and Honours received by him:

  • 2007 - In May 2007, Ruit was appointed an Honorary Officer of the Order of Australia, "for service to humanity by establishing eye care services in Nepal and surrounding countries, and for his work in teaching and training surgeons, and technical innovation".
  • 2006 - In June 2006, he was awarded the Ramon Magsaysay Award.
  • 2010 - Asteroid 83362 Sandukruit, discovered by Bill Yeung in 2001, was named in his honor.The official naming citation was published by the Minor Planet Center on 30 March 2010.
  • 2015 - On December 17, 2015, he was appointed Member of the National Order of Merit of Bhutan [in Gold].
  • 2016 - On October 27, 2016, he received an Asia Game Changer Award from the Asia Society "for bringing the gifts of sight, and productive life, to those most in need."
  • 2018 - In 2018, the Government of India honoured him with the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian honour.
  • 2020 - In 2020 September, Nepal Government announced Dr Sanduk Ruit, ,will be honoured with Suprasiddha Prabal Janasewashree (first).
